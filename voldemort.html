Tom Riddle

Tom Riddle, later known as Lord Voldemort, is portrayed as a brilliant yet deeply disturbed wizard whose intelligence, charm, and ambition are corrupted by a profound fear of death and an obsessive desire for power and control. Born to a loveless union and raised in an orphanage, Riddle grows up emotionally detached, manipulative, and unable to form genuine human bonds, viewing others as tools to be used rather than people to be valued.
Exceptionally talented at Hogwarts, especially in magic that pushes ethical boundaries, he becomes fascinated with immortality and purity of blood, developing a belief in his own superiority and a contempt for weakness.
His inability to understand love, empathy, or sacrifice shapes his transformation into Voldemort, leading him to create Horcruxes, build a following through fear, and pursue domination over both the magical and non-magical worlds.
Riddle embodies the theme of potential corrupted by choice, showing how intelligence without conscience and ambition without humanity can lead to moral decay and self-destruction.
